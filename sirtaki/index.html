The Story of Sirtaki

The Sirtaki was created by composer Mikis Theodorakis for the 1964 film "Zorba the Greek." This captivating dance combines two traditional Greek dances:

  • Hasapiko - The slow, graceful beginning
  • Hasaposerviko - The fast, energetic finale

What makes Sirtaki unique is its progressive tempo - starting slow and building to an exhilarating crescendo that captures the spirit of Greek celebration.

Two-Person Coordination

ü§ù Synchronization Tips

  • Start together on count 1
  • Match your partner's tempo
  • Use peripheral vision to stay in sync
  • Practice with a metronome first

üë• Leadership Roles

  • Right dancer typically leads tempo changes
  • Left dancer follows and maintains connection
  • Switch roles during practice
  • Communicate through subtle cues

üîó Maintaining Connection

  • Keep hands firm but not tight on shoulders
  • Maintain consistent spacing
  • Move as one unit
  • Don't break formation during kicks

üí¨ Communication

  • Use eye contact for timing
  • Practice verbal cues for tempo changes
  • Develop non-verbal signals
  • Give encouraging feedback

Not keeping weight on ball of foot

Stay light and ready to move. Avoid flat-footed steps.

Moving too fast too early

Respect the progressive tempo. Start slow and build gradually.

Losing connection with partner

Maintain shoulder contact throughout the dance.

Poor timing with music

Listen to the rhythm and count beats carefully.

Stiff upper body movement

Keep shoulders relaxed and allow natural movement.
