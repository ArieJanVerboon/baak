About Natura 2000 Network

Natura 2000 is the largest coordinated network of protected areas in the world, stretching across all 27 EU countries, covering over 18% of the EU's land area and almost 10% of its marine territory.

üê¶ Avian Diversity in the Cyclades

The Cyclades are a globally important area for bird conservation with:

  • 133 protected bird species across all sites
  • 42 breeding species (21 residents, 21 migrants)
  • Critical habitat for 85% of global Eleonora's falcon population
  • Major breeding colonies of Mediterranean endemic seabirds
  • Important migration route for hundreds of species

üåä Marine Protection in the Cyclades

The Cyclades host some of the Mediterranean's most important marine protected areas, including:

  • Critical habitats for the endangered Mediterranean monk seal
  • Extensive Posidonia meadows - underwater forests essential for marine life
  • Breeding areas for sea turtles and seabirds
  • Endemic species found nowhere else on Earth
  • Important feeding grounds for dolphins and marine mammals

Conservation Highlights

Gyaros hosts approximately 70 Mediterranean monk seals - one of the largest populations worldwide. In 2025, the first green turtle nest was recorded in the Cyclades at Naxos, marking a significant conservation milestone.
