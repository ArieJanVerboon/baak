Henry Miller's Journey Through Greece

The Colossus of Maroussi

Follow Miller's transformative 1939 journey

Year: 1939
Duration: 9 months
Context: Pre-WWII escape from Paris at Lawrence Durrell's invitation
1

Athens

Arrival and meeting George Katsimbalis

Miller arrives at Piraeus and meets the charismatic poet George Katsimbalis, the "Colossus" of his book title.

"The light of Greece opened my eyes, penetrated my pores, expanded my whole being"
2

Delos

Spiritual rebirth experience

On the sacred uninhabited island of Delos, birthplace of Apollo, Miller experiences a spiritual rebirth.

"Marvelous things happen to one in Greece—marvelous good things which can happen to one nowhere else on earth"
3

Mykonos

Dazzling light and island life

Miller is struck by the brilliant clarity of Cycladic light and the simplicity of island life.

"The dazzling clarity of the light, the sea's brilliance, and the simplicity of island life"
4

Poros

Timeless, mystical Greece

The dreamlike approach by boat to Poros fills Miller with a sense of eternal, mystical Greece.

"To sail slowly through the streets of Poros is to recapture the joy of passing through the neck of the womb"
5

Hydra

Artistic inspiration and serenity

Miller finds peace and artistic inspiration in the island's cobblestone alleys and timeless beauty.

"A moment which endures beyond world wars"
6

Crete/Knossos

Sitting on the Throne of Minos

Miller visits the Palace of Knossos and sits on King Minos' throne, feeling connected to ancient civilizations.

"Seated on King Minos throne I felt closer to Montezuma than to Homer"

Henry Miller's Transformative Journey

In 1939, American writer Henry Miller embarked on a life-changing journey through Greece at the invitation of Lawrence Durrell. This 9-month odyssey became the foundation for his masterpiece "The Colossus of Maroussi" (1941).

Click on the markers to explore each location and discover how Greece transformed Miller's perspective on life, art, and spirituality.
